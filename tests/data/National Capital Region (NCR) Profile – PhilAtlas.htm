The National Capital Region, officially designated as NCR, is an administrative region in the Philippines occupying the central section of Luzon. It covers 1 municipality, as well as 16 highly urbanized cities. The regional center is the City of Manila.

Its population as determined by the 2020 Census was 13,484,462. This represented 21.68% of the overall population of the Luzon island group, or 12.37% of the entire population of the Philippines. Based on these figures, the population density is computed at 21,765 inhabitants per square kilometer or 56,371 inhabitants per square mile.

Map

National Capital Region is bordered, clockwise from the North, by Central Luzon, CALABARZON, Laguna de Bay, and the Manila Bay.

Demographics

Population by age group

According to the 2015 Census, the age group with the highest population in National Capital Region is 20 to 24, with 1,349,005 individuals. Conversely, the age group with the lowest population is 80 and over, with 76,146 individuals.

details

Combining age groups together, those aged 14 and below, consisting of the young dependent population which include infants/babies, children and young adolescents/teenagers, make up an aggregate of 27.18% (3,499,754). Those aged 15 up to 64, roughly, the economically active population and actual or potential members of the work force, constitute a total of 68.89% (8,871,449). Finally, old dependent population consisting of the senior citizens, those aged 65 and over, total 3.93% (506,050) in all.

The computed Age Dependency Ratios mean that among the population of National Capital Region (NCR), there are 39 youth dependents to every 100 of the working age population; there are 6 aged/senior citizens to every 100 of the working population; and overall, there are 45 dependents (young and old-age) to every 100 of the working population.

The median age of 26 indicates that half of the entire population of National Capital Region (NCR) are aged less than 26 and the other half are over the age of 26.

Historical population

The population of National Capital Region grew from 328,939 in 1903 to 13,484,462 in 2020, an increase of 13,155,523 people over the course of 117 years. The latest census figures in 2020 denote a positive growth rate of 0.97%, or an increase of 607,209 people, from the previous population of 12,877,253 in 2015.
